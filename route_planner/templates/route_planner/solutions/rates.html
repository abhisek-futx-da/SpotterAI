Spotter's AI analyzes real-time prices at every station within your corridor, then balances the price-per-gallon savings against the cost of any deviation from your route. It factors in your tank capacity, current fuel level, MPG, and per-stop purchase minimums to find the sequence of stops that minimizes your total fuel cost — not just the cheapest station on the map.
Spotter aggregates live price feeds from 2,400+ stations across all 50 states. Data is updated multiple times daily. For time-critical operations, we recommend running the optimizer within 2 hours of departure to get the most accurate pricing for your specific route.
The corridor width defines how far off your direct route Spotter will look for fuel stations. A 25-mile corridor means we'll consider stations up to 25 miles on either side of your route. Wider corridors find more options but may include stations that add meaningful detour time. Most drivers find 20–35 miles optimal for long-haul routes.
The current optimizer is designed for point-to-point routes with intermediate fuel stops. Multi-drop delivery route optimization is on our roadmap for Q3. For now, you can run separate optimizations for each leg of a multi-stop trip and the fuel planning will remain accurate for each segment.
